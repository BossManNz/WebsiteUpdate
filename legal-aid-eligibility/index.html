Legal Aid Eligibility

Important: Eligibility depends on more than income, including your assets.

Even if you are over the threshold, you may still be to get legal aid on hardship grounds.

What you will need
  • Evidence of your income (for example payslips, or a benefit statement found in the myMSD portal).
  • If you have a partner, you will need to provide evidence of both your income and your partner’s income.
  • Any information that supports hardship, for example, a breakdown of how your income is spent.
What you need to know
  • Legal aid is considered a loan, this means you may need to pay some or all of the costs incurred under your grant back.
  • If you are above the below thresholds, there is no guarantee that your Legal Aid application will be granted.
  • In some circumstances, Legal Aid will let you pay back your legal fees in instalments. You could be required to start paying back your legal fees with interim repayments while your proceedings are ongoing.
How to apply for legal aid
  • You need to instruct a lawyer to get legal aid.
  • You need a lawyer to help you fill out a legal aid application, and the application requires your lawyer’s signature.
  • Your lawyer will submit your legal aid application for you and advise you of the result and any repayment amounts required.
