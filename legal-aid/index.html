Legal Aid

Legal aid can help with the cost of a lawyer in some situations. This page explains the published income thresholds and includes an income checker to help you estimate where you sit.

FAQs Income checker Thresholds Need representation?

FAQ: Common questions asked about what is needed to bring to an appointment with a lawyer, and what to know about legal aid.

What you will need to apply
  • Evidence of your income (for example payslips, or a benefit statement found in the myMSD portal).
  • If you have a partner, you will need to provide evidence of both your income and your partner’s income.
  • If you own a business, information about your business finanaicals.
  • If you have an interest in a trust, or if a trust owes you money, a copy of the trust deed.
  • Any information that supports hardship, for example, a breakdown of how your income is spent.
What you need to know to apply
  • Legal aid is considered a loan, this means you may need to pay some or all of the costs incurred under your grant back.
  • If you are above the published thresholds, there is no guarantee that your Legal Aid application will be granted.
  • In some circumstances, Legal Aid may let you pay back your legal fees in instalments. You could be required to start paying back your legal fees with interim repayments while your proceedings are ongoing.
  • If you own a home, you will need to sign a statutory land authority form, and a caveat may be registered over your home as a security for repaying your grant of legal aid.
How to apply for legal aid
  • You need to instruct a lawyer to get legal aid.
  • You need a lawyer to help you fill out a legal aid application, and the application requires your lawyer’s signature.
  • Your lawyer will submit your legal aid application for you and advise you of the result and any repayment amounts required.
